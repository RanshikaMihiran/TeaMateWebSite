Research Gap

Following areas are the research gaps found in most of the recent researches.

Identification & classification

The primary research gap in tea production lies in the lack of a holistic, automated system that can accurately predict and optimize critical factors like water levels and quality assessment across various tea leaf types while adapting to environmental fluctuations. Currently, water levels are checked manually, an inaccurate method that, along with existing single-factor approaches—such as focusing only on moisture content or specific leaf varieties—fails to provide comprehensive resource management. Additionally, there is no robust model in Sri Lanka for predicting tea leaf prices based on water content, grade, and texture, or for understanding farmer churn behavior. Addressing these gaps calls for a comprehensive, data-driven approach that leverages machine learning to streamline quality checks, optimize water use, and enhance supply chain insights for the tea industry.

Severity assessment

The severity assessment highlights several critical gaps in tea production, with the most urgent being the need for automated, accurate methods for water level checking and quality assessment. Currently, manual water level checks are unreliable, affecting tea quality and resource efficiency. Similarly, the lack of automated quality control leads to labor-intensive, error-prone evaluations that undermine production consistency. Additionally, there is an absence of a tailored pricing model for Sri Lanka’s tea industry, crucial for stabilizing farmer incomes and understanding churn behavior. Environmental adaptability and machine learning-driven data collection are also essential to ensure accurate measurements and predictive capabilities, but they require advanced technological resources. Addressing these issues with automation and data-driven solutions would significantly enhance efficiency, quality, and sustainability in tea production.

Information sharing

Effective information sharing in tea production is essential for improving decision-making, operational efficiency, and overall quality. By creating a centralized system that integrates data on water levels, leaf quality, environmental factors, and market dynamics, tea producers and stakeholders can achieve real-time insights and actionable feedback. Automated data sharing through connected devices and cloud systems allows for timely adjustments, such as optimizing water usage based on leaf type and environmental conditions, which improves resource management. Additionally, sharing predictive models on leaf prices and churn behavior can help stabilize the supply chain, as producers and farmers gain a clearer view of market trends and potential challenges. Clear, consistent communication ensures that all stakeholders—from factory operators to suppliers—remain informed, enabling a cohesive approach to maintaining quality and sustainability in tea production.

Research Problem & Solution

Proposed Problem

How do environmental factors affect water level estimation in tea leaves?

The research problem addressed in this study is the need for an automated, accurate system for assessing water content and quality of fresh tea factories, while accounting for environmental fluctuations that impact leaves intergrity and production outcome.Traditional methods for determining water levels and leaves quality are manual, subjective and inconsistent , leading to inefficiencies in the drying process, potential degradation in leaves quality, and variable products standards.Furthermore, the lack of a systemaric approach to tea leaves pricing considering factores such as grade and water level creates pricing disparties and supply chain disruptiions, which affect fair compensation for farmers and stability in production.Therefore, this research seeks to develop a Smart Water Level Testing Device that intergrates sensor technology and machine learning to provide real time insights, enabling tea factories to optimize processing, ensure premium product quality and facilitate equitable pricing within the tea industry.


Product Demonstration - Solution

Proposed Solution

Our proposed solution, Tea Mate, is a comprehensive system aimed at enhancing the quality and efficiency of tea production through intelligent monitoring and management. This solution addresses essential factors that impact tea quality and market readiness, such as water content, storage conditions, price forecasting, and quality control. Tea Mate leverages IoT sensors to gather real-time environmental data, monitoring key metrics like temperature, humidity, and moisture levels to maintain optimal storage conditions for tea leaves. Advanced data analytics and machine learning models are also integrated into the system to analyze trends and make accurate price forecasts, helping producers make informed decisions that align with market demands. Additionally, Tea Mate uses image processing and environmental monitoring to assess tea leaf quality consistently, detecting issues such as spoilage or infestation early on. This proactive approach reduces waste and ensures that only high-quality tea reaches the market. Overall, Tea Mate serves as a complete solution that not only streamlines production but also provides a reliable, data-driven foundation for decision-making in the tea industry. By combining automation with predictive insights, it enhances quality control and efficiency, benefiting both producers and consumers while promoting sustainable and profitable tea production

Research Objectives

Water Level Identification in Fresh Tea Leaves

The quality of the tea leaves used in production is of paramount importance in determining the final product's market value. High-quality tea leaves, characterized by their optimal chemical composition and physical attributes, are essential for producing teas that meet international standards and consumer expectations. Among the various factors that influence tea leaf quality, the water content during processing is particularly crucial. The moisture level in tea leaves affects not only the physical properties of the leaves but also the biochemical reactions that occur during the different stages of production, such as withering, rolling, fermentation, and drying.

Price Prediction of Tea Leaves & Farmer Churn Prediction

The tea industry in Sri Lanka faces challenges in accurately determining the price of fresh tea leaves, a process crucial for ensuring fair compensation for farmers and maintaining the quality of tea production. This thesis presents the development of a sophisticated price prediction system for fresh tea leaves, incorporating critical factors such as water level, region, quality, and texture, alongside a farmer churn behavior prediction model. The primary objective is to create a reliable and precise price prediction model that considers the multifaceted elements influencing tea leaf quality and market value. Additionally, the study aims to analyze the factors contributing to farmer churn, providing a comprehensive approach to retaining essential agricultural contributors within the tea supply chain.

Identification of Fresh Tea Leaves Quality

The system undergoes rigorous testing to evaluate its performance in accurately identifying key quality indicators such as Tea types, colors, shapes, sizes, humadity, temperature, light intensity and rainy level and structural integrity of tea leaves. Tests are conducted under varying environmental conditions and with different tea leaf samples to assess robustness and generalization capabilities. The developed system demonstrates high accuracy and efficiency in fresh tea leaf quality assessment, achieving reliable classification results across multiple quality criteria. Real-time processing capabilities enable timely feedback to tea factory operators, facilitating proactive quality management and process optimization.

Inventory Condition Management

A new paradigm shift to the Internet of Things (IoT) offers new possibilities for improvement of inventory management of various kinds of products, including tea. The IoT technology leads to the deployment of constantly monitoring and regulating the impact of environmental concerns through devices and sensors. These devices are able to pick-accomplish temperature, relative humidity and light exposure data continually and control the storage environment according to predetermined specifications. This automation minimizes the effects of human interference to the means of exerting tighter control over the storing conditions. In the case of tea industry the adoption of an IoT-based smart inventory management system can enable proper storage of tea leaves and can results in increased yield and quality of the product.

Methodology

Figure 2. High Level Architecture of the system.

The data collection methodology for this research is designed to enhance tea factory operations using Smart IoT solutions, focusing on real-time monitoring and management of critical environmental and operational factors to optimize tea production processes. Key components of the methodology include developing a Smart Water Level Testing Device and integrating IoT-based systems for water level recommendations, fresh tea leaf quality checks, price prediction, farmer churn analysis, and inventory condition management. For water level recommendations, real-time data will be sourced from IoT sensors and weather stations, capturing factory temperature, humidity, and water levels essential for understanding and optimizing leaf moisture during processing. Fresh tea leaf quality is assessed through IoT-enabled cameras, spectrometers, and historical data on leaf characteristics and environmental factors, which will be processed with machine learning algorithms to ensure high-quality production. Price and churn predictions leverage data from market databases, factories, and cooperatives, using historical trends and supply chain information to forecast market prices and predict farmer churn. Inventory condition management employs IoT sensors within storage facilities to track temperature, humidity, and light exposure, ensuring tea leaves are stored optimally, with real-time alerts and predictive analytics assisting managers in preventing spoilage and stockouts. Data preprocessing, including cleaning, normalization, and feature extraction, ensures accuracy and reliability, enhancing the predictive models across all areas. This integrated approach enables informed decision-making, fostering efficient, high-quality tea production, and provides actionable insights into market trends and farmer behavior, benefiting the entire tea industry.
